Understanding People's Brain

Psychology for me, is a very precious passion, as I have struggled for a long time finding my own passion.
While my peers were able to choose their university majors and future careers, I was the only one who fell behind, and kept on searching for my own.
When I encountered Freud’s psychoanalysis, a spark lightened in my head.
That was the first academic subject that I thought I would be able to pursue my dream in.

Interviewing Dr. Guy Sutton

A world-renowned neuroscientist Dr. Guy Sutton provided a number of exciting lectures and workshops for both students and staff, on topics including “Brains for Medics,” “Careers in Biomedical Science,” “Nerve Cells, Bionic Arms and Mind Reading” and “The Brain and Crime.” Our psychology society had the chance to interview him about the mysteries of the brain.

Exploring Psychology

I designed the “Exploring Psychology” co-curricular activity, which is targeted to Year 10-11 students who are passionate about psychology like me

I gave lectures about intriguing psychological topics, for instance, “Freudian Psychology” and “Developmental Psychology” to y10-11 and helped them with academics

I also devised several forms of art therapy, for example, “The Never-Sent Letter”, which helped them to relieve academic stress from IGCSEs
